The quadratic equation (from Latin quadraus 'square') is an equation that can be rearanged in standard form as ax² + bx + c = 0, where the variable x represents an unknown number , and a, b, c represents known numbers, where a ≠ 0. (If a = 0 and b ≠ 0 then the equation is linear, not quadratic.) The number a, b, c are the coeffeicients of the equation and may be dinstiguished by repectively calling them, the quadratic coefficient, the linear coefficients and the constant coefficients or free term. The value that sasitfy the equation are called solutions of the equation, and the roots or zeros of the quadratic function on its left-side. A quadratic equation has at most two solutions. If there is only one solution, it is a doule root , or two complex solutions that are complex conjuates of each other. A quadratic equation always has two roots, if copmlex roots are included and a double root is counteed for two. A quadratic equation can be factored into a equivalent equation.
